Recipe #1

Scratchcake

  • Time: 30min
  • Serves: about 8 pieces
Ingredients Quantity
Butter 100 g
Eggs 2
Powdered sugar 3 dl
Cocoa 3 msk
Vanilla sugar 2 teaspons
Wheat flour 11/2 dl

Step 1: Set oven to 200 ° C

Step 2: Melt the butter in a saucepan, allow to cool slightly.

Step 3: Whisk together eggs and sugar (do not use an electric whisk).

Step 4: Mix cocoa, vanilla sugar and wheat flour in a bowl and stir in the egg mixture.

Step 5: Add the melted butter and gently mix together to form a smooth batter, then pour over the butter in a greased and breaded spring form with removable edge, 24 cm in diameter (for 8 pieces).

Step 6: Bake in the middle of the oven for about 10-15 minutes (increase or decrease the time if necessary depending on how sticky / chewy you want it).

Recipe #2

Pink princess roll cake

  • Time: Over 60min
  • Serves: 10
Ingredients Quantity
Eggs 3
Sugar 11/2 cup
Wheat flour 2 dl
Baking powder 1 teaspoon
Whipped cream 2 cups
Vanilla powder 2 krm
Icing sugar 1 tablespoon
Raspberry jam 11/2 dl
Pink marzipan lid 1 (about 200g)
Fresh raspberries 125 g

Step 1:Set oven to 250 ° C.

Step 2:Whisk eggs and powdered sugar puffy with electric whisk.

Step 3:Mix wheat flour and baking powder. Carefully turn down the batter.

Step 4:Spread the batter on a baking sheet covered with paper. Bake in the middle of the oven for about 5 minutes. Allow to cool completely.

Step 5:Support the bottom of a baking sheet paper. Carefully pull off the top paper.

Step 6:Whip whipped cream. Mix with vanilla powder and icing sugar.

Step 7:Wide raspberry jam on the cold bottom. Wide half of the vanilla cream.

Step 8:Roll up from one long side. Roll in the marzipan lid. If necessary, cut off excess marzipan and cut away the edges.

Step 9:Powder over icing sugar just before serving. Invite with the remaining vanilla cream and fresh raspberries.

Recipe #3

Tiger Mousse

  • Time: Over60min
  • Serves: 4 servings
Ingredients Quantity
Dark chocolate 100 g
Whipped cream 2 cups
Egg yolks 2
Whisky 1 tbsp
Quart gourmet 1 dl

Step 1:Break the chocolate into pieces and melt it in a water bath or at low power in the microwave.

Step 2:Whip the cream, but not too hard. Stir in one egg at a time in the chocolate. Also stir the cream down a little at a time. Season with cognac.

Step 3:Turn mousse and quince gourmet into four glasses (for 4 port). Stick in the mousse with a spoon to mix it into a pattern. Garnish with chocolate chips.

Step 4:Leave the mousse in the fridge for a few hours to serve.
